Biography

Terence D. Dores Cruz is a PhD Candidate in the department of Organizational Sciences (Faculty of Social Sciences) at the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. Terence currently studies gossip in Bianca Beersma’s ERC-Consolidator Grant Project FORCE-OF-GOSSIP. Terence is also the student-representative for the Kurt Lewin Institute. Terence holds a research master’s degree from the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam (Amsterdam Cooperation Lab) and bachelor’s degree from the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam.

Terence’s main research interests are human cooperation (and non-cooperation) and how cooperative behavior is shaped by reputation and gossip. Terence takes an interdisciplinary approach building on evolutionary, social, and organizational psychology employing interactive experiments, vignettes, surveys, experience sampling, and diary studies to answer questions about gossip, reputation, and cooperation.

Terence’s current work focuses on the cooperative versus competitive functions of gossip and how gossip can help or hinder reputation-based cooperation (short clip). Terence investigates proself versus prosocial motives for sharing gossip, how receivers of gossip react to gossip in terms of cooperation with gossipers and targets of gossip, and how receivers attribute motives to gossipers which can shape their reaction to gossip.
